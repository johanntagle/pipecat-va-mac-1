### Beef Salpicao
**Portuguese-Spanish Fusion Favorite**
A beloved dish from the Iberian Peninsula featuring tender beef in a bold garlic-soy glaze.

**Key Components:**
- Premium beef cubes (tenderloin or ribeye)
- Generous garlic chips (crispy and sweet)
- Worcestershire-soy glaze
- Touch of butter for richness
- Served sizzling hot

**Flavor Profile:** Garlicky, savory, slightly sweet, umami-rich
**Texture:** Tender beef, crispy garlic contrast
**Origin:** Portuguese via Spanish colonial connections
**Serving Style:** Hot plate or cast iron skillet
**Perfect For:** Garlic lovers, those wanting hearty comfort food
**Wine Pairing:** Full-bodied red wine

### Callos con Garbanzos
**Traditional Spanish Comfort Food**
For the adventurous eater, this slow-cooked tripe stew is a beloved classic in Spain.

**What Is It?**
A rich, hearty stew featuring:
- Beef tripe (stomach lining) - tender after slow cooking
- Chickpeas (garbanzos) for texture and body
- Chorizo and morcilla (blood sausage) for depth
- Slow-cooked in paprika-rich tomato sauce
- Bay leaves, peppers, and Spanish spices

**Flavor Profile:** Rich, smoky, deeply savory, warming
**Texture:** Tender, gelatinous tripe with firm chickpeas
**Cooking Time:** Slow-cooked for hours until tender
**Perfect For:** Adventurous diners, those who love nose-to-tail dining, cold weather
**Wine Pairing:** Robust Spanish red wine
**Cultural Note:** This is true comfort food in Spain, often enjoyed in tascas (traditional taverns)

**Not Sure About Tripe?** 
Ask your server for a small taste - many first-timers become converts! The long cooking process transforms tripe into something completely different from what you might expect.
