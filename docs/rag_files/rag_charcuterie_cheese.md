## CHEESE PLATTERS

### Tabla de Quesos (Cheese Platter)
**A Spanish Cheese Journey**
Explore the diversity of Spanish cheese-making with our selection of artisanal cheeses from different regions.

**What's Included:**
Our rotating selection typically features 4-5 Spanish cheeses, which may include:

**Manchego** (La Mancha region)
- Spain's most famous cheese
- Sheep's milk, firm texture
- Aged varieties: Semi-curado (3-6 months), Curado (6-12 months), Viejo (12+ months)
- Flavor: Nutty, slightly tangy, buttery
- The longer the aging, the more intense the flavor

**Cabrales** (Asturias)
- Blue cheese made in caves
- Cow's milk (sometimes mixed with sheep or goat)
- Intense, pungent, creamy
- Spain's answer to Roquefort
- Not for the faint of heart!

**Mahón** (Menorca)
- Cow's milk cheese
- Orange rind from paprika rubbing
- Firm, slightly salty
- Flavors: Buttery with a tangy finish

**Tetilla** (Galicia)
- Named for its breast-like shape
- Soft, creamy cow's milk cheese
- Mild, buttery, melts in your mouth
- Perfect for blue cheese skeptics

**Idiazábal** (Basque Country)
- Smoked sheep's milk cheese
- Firm texture
- Distinctive smoky flavor
- Nutty and slightly spicy

**Served With:**
- Quince paste (membrillo) - the classic Spanish pairing
- Honey (local Spanish honey when available)
- Marcona almonds - roasted, salted Spanish almonds
- Fresh and dried fruits
- Artisan crackers and bread
- Fig jam

**Serves:** 2-3 people as an appetizer, or as part of a larger spread
**Perfect For:** 
- Cheese lovers exploring Spanish varieties
- Pre-dinner socializing
- Wine tasting accompaniment
- Romantic date nights

**Wine Pairing:**
- Spanish red wines (Rioja, Ribera del Duero)
- Cava (Spanish sparkling wine)
- Sherry (particularly Fino or Amontillado)
- Spanish whites (Albariño, Verdejo)

**Cheese Education:**
Your server can guide you through the selection, explaining origins, aging processes, and flavor profiles. Don't be shy to ask!

## CURED MEAT PLATTERS

### Tabla de Embutidos (Cold Cuts Platter)
**Spain's Cured Meat Masterclass**
Spanish charcuterie (embutidos) is an art form refined over centuries. This platter showcases the variety and quality Spain is famous for.

**Featured Selections (4-5 varieties):**

**Jamón Ibérico** - The Crown Jewel
- From acorn-fed Iberian pigs (bellota grade when available)
- Dark red color, intense marbling
- Cured 24-36 months
- Melt-in-your-mouth texture
- Nutty, complex flavor from acorn diet
- Sliced paper-thin by hand
- Why it's special: These pigs roam oak forests eating acorns, which flavors the meat

**Jamón Serrano** - The Classic
- Mountain ham from white pigs
- Cured 12-18 months
- Lighter color than Ibérico
- Clean, savory, slightly salty
- More accessible price point, still exceptional quality

**Chorizo Ibérico**
- Cured pork sausage with pimentón (Spanish paprika)
- Two styles: Picante (spicy) or Dulce (sweet/mild)
- Deep red color from paprika
- Smoky, garlicky, with a hint of wine
- Firm texture, rich flavor

**Salchichón**
- Spanish version of salami
- Peppercorn-studded
- Milder than Italian salami
- Subtle spices, clean pork flavor
- Often with black pepper you can see

**Lomo Ibérico**
- Cured pork loin
- Lean, tender
- Delicate flavor
- Sliced thin
- For those who prefer less fat

**Morcilla** (when available)
- Spanish blood sausage
- Made with rice, onions, spices, and pork blood
- Rich, earthy, surprisingly mild
- Spreadable texture
- For adventurous eaters

**Served With:**
- Picos (Spanish breadsticks)
- Pa amb tomàquet (tomato rubbed bread)
- Cornichons and pickled vegetables
- Olives (various Spanish varieties)
- Extra virgin Spanish olive oil
- Fresh tomatoes

**Serves:** 2-3 people as appetizer
**Slicing:** All meats are sliced to order - never pre-sliced
**Storage:** We store our jamón at cellar temperature and slice fresh for optimal flavor

**Wine Pairing:**
- Tempranillo
- Rioja Crianza or Reserva
- Cava or Spanish sparkling wine
- Spanish beer also works beautifully

**The Art of Jamón:**
In Spain, jamón carving is a respected skill. Our staff is trained in proper slicing technique to ensure each slice has the right thickness and fat distribution.

## THE SPANISH CHARCUTERIE TRADITION

**Why Spain?**
Spain has perfected the art of curing meat over centuries, thanks to:
- Climate: Dry mountain air perfect for curing
- Pig breeds: Indigenous Iberian pigs with unique genetics
- Acorn forests: Dehesa ecosystems where pigs roam
- Generational knowledge: Techniques passed down through families
- Protected designations: Strict quality controls and regional protections

**Jamón Aging:**
The longer a jamón cures, the more complex its flavor:
- 12 months: Basic quality
- 18-24 months: Standard excellent quality
- 24-36 months: Premium, complex flavors
- 36+ months: Reserve, intense, prized

**How to Eat Jamón Like a Spaniard:**
1. Take a slice with your fingers (it's encouraged!)
2. Let it warm on your palm briefly (brings out flavors)
3. Place on your tongue and let it melt slowly
4. Notice how the fat melts and releases flavors
5. Appreciate the nutty, sweet notes from the acorn diet
